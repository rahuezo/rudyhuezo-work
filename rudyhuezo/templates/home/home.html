About

I'm currently working as an applications programmer at the Department of Environmental Science and Policy at UC Davis. My work focuses on the automation of data collection, sorting, analysis, and visualization.

My passion for programming began in 2007 when I was introduced to HTML and Flash animations. I spent the next 5 years making small static websites, games, and simulations using Flash.

I was formally introduced to programming languages in 2012 when I took my first Python course at UC Davis. From that point on I have worked with various colleagues and professors using a wide array of programming and markup languages, and some web development frameworks: Python, Matlab, R, C/C++/C#, JavaScript, JQuery, p5.js, matter.js, kinetic.js, HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap.

For fun, I love to exercise (hiking, lifting weights, running), I enjoy eating and cooking good food and tasting great beers. I enjoy photography, videography, playing guitar, and fashion. I am interested in deep and thought-provoking conversations and passionate about science and technology.

If you're a person or a small business wanting to take your services or ideas online, you can hire me to help you make your visions a reality.
